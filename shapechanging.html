Shape-Changing Break Reminders

An exploratory study with people with Repetitive Strain Injury.

This project is in progress as a part of my graduate thesis. This project is in process and I will be happy to discuss it in person.

Quick Summary

Prolonged sitting due to computer work is a major contributor to a sedentary lifestyle. This increases longtime health risks and also causes repetitive strain injury in many people resulting in chronic pain in their wrist, arms, shoulders or back that limits their normal activity. There is strong evidence that taking micro-breaks every hour; as short a minute can substantially help the body to recover from the damage caused by inactivity. However, these breaks are often untimely, not context-aware, and disruptive to workflow.

Calm technologies like shape-changing devices have been a growing area of research in the field of Human-Computer Interaction. This project focusses ongoing in the context where RSI occurs, understanding the lived experience of these people, and including their voices early on in the design process. This was done by conducting an interactive session with 11 people who have RSI. The session was designed to equip them to think about shape-changing devices: moving gradually from a descriptive interview to a design critique to an imaginative codesign.
